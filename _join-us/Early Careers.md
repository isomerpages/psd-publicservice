<h2><strong>Your Public Service Career Begins Here</strong></h2>
<h4>Shape Singapore's Future - and Yours.</h4>
<p></p>
<p>In the Singapore Public Service, every role is more than just a job—it’s
an opportunity to build a better tomorrow, creating meaningful impact that
reaches beyond yourself.</p>
<p>Join a team that tackles current and future challenges on a national scale.
Whether you’re seeking your first full-time role or an internship, the
Singapore Public Service offers diverse opportunities to shape not just
the future of our nation, but your own journey as well.</p>

<h4><strong><u>What To Expect</u></strong></h4>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Grow and learn: </strong>Build your career through performance-based
progression, with opportunities to specialise, lead, and gain new skills
through courses, job rotations, and attachments in different sectors.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Get career support: </strong>Receive guidance from career coaches
and access online courses to help you grow.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Flexible work arrangements</strong>: These include flexi-load
(e.g. part time), flexi-place (e.g. telecommuting) and flexi-time (e.g.
staggered hours), subject to the operational requirements of the role and
public agency. A network of co-working spaces around Singapore is also
available for public officers to use.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Competitive remuneration and benefits</strong>: Access a wide
range of leave benefits for personal (e.g. vacation, study, volunteering,
marriage, medical) and family needs (e.g. maternity / paternity, child
care and family care)</p>
<p></p>
<p>Learn more - <a href="https://www.careers.gov.sg/join-us/faqs/career-progression-benefits/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Career Progression and Benefits</a>
</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h4><strong><u>FAQs</u></strong></h4>
<div data-type="detailGroup" class="isomer-accordion isomer-accordion-white">
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary><strong>Q: What is the difference between a Statutory Board and a Ministry?</strong>
</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p>The Singapore Public Service offers a wide range of career opportunities
and employs about 152,000 public officers who work in 16 Ministries and
more than 50 Statutory Boards. Within the Public Service is the Civil Service,
comprising about 86,000 officers working in the Ministries.
<br>
</p>
<p>Statutory boards (such as HDB, CPF Board, IRAS, etc) have their own recruitment
and human resource management practices. They are legally distinct employers
from the Civil Service.
<br>
</p>
<p>Whether your passion lies in education, supporting families, keeping Singapore
safe, or addressing climate change, we have a role for you.</p>
<p></p>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary><strong>Q: Does the Public Service accept candidates from any discipline or do I have to specialise in a particular field?</strong>
</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p>The Public Service generally accepts candidates from all disciplines.
However, for positions in specialised fields such as accountancy, legal
and medical services, a professional degree in the relevant field of study
is required.</p>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary><strong>Q: How are starting salaries determined in the Public Service?</strong>
</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p>Your starting salary is be based on:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Your credentials (such as your work experience and skills)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The demands of the job (such as the type of work and scheme of service
that you are appointed to)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The prevailing labour market conditions</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary><strong>Q: Is it true that the Public Service only recruits graduates with excellent academic qualifications?</strong>
</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p>The Public Service offers a wide range of jobs with different requirements.
We take into account a variety of factors when considering candidates for
our roles such as relevant skills that align with the job’s requirements.
Officers from diverse backgrounds serving in these roles bring their own
skills, experiences and perspectives to their job.</p>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary><strong>&nbsp;Q: When can I apply for an internship or entry-level position?</strong>
</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p><strong>For Entry-Level Positions:</strong> If you are a final-year undergraduate
graduating soon, you may start applying for any on-going recruitment exercises.
If you are found suitable, you may be given a provisional offer of appointment.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>For Internships: </strong>Applications for internships are open
year-round and are typically posted three to six months before the start
date. You may find internships that are currently recruiting via our job
portal.</p>
<p></p>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary><strong>Q: Can non-Singaporeans apply for internships and full-time jobs?</strong>
</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p>Yes, non-Singaporeans can apply and be considered for jobs in the Public
Service. Non-citizen officers generally serve in positions that require
skills that are in shortage among our local workforce, such as engineering
and information technology; and where the security concerns can be managed
adequately.</p>
</div>
</details>
<details class="isomer-details">
<summary><strong>Q: Does the Public Service provide any scholarships?</strong>
</summary>
<div data-type="detailsContent" class="isomer-details-content">
<p>The Public Service offers undergraduate and post-graduate scholarships.
You may find out more at <a rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">psc.gov.sg/scholarships</a>
</p>
</div>
</details>
